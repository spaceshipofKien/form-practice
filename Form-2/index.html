Richard Feynman

The American theoretical physicist

He is best known for his work in the path integral formulation of quantum mechanics, the theory of quantum electrodynamics, the physics of the superfluidity of supercooled liquid helium, and in particle physics, for which he proposed the parton model.

Here's a timeline of Richard Feynman's life:

  • 1918 - Born in New York, USA

  • 1939 - Feynman received a bachelor's degree[36] and was named a Putnam Fellow

  • 1942 - Feynman received a PhD from Princeton

  • 1941 - Feynman was recruited by Robert R. Wilson, who was working on means to produce enriched uranium for use in an atomic bomb, as part of what would become the Manhattan Project

  • 1948 - Feynman presented his work to his peers at the Pocono Conference
